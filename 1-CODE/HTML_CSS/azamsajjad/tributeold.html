Dr. Pervez Hoodbhoy

The only voice of reason in Pakistan

Pervez Amirali Hoodbhoy is a Pakistani nuclear physicist and activist who serves as a professor at the Forman Christian College and previously taught physics at the Quaid-e-Azam University.

  • 1950 - Born in Karachi, Pakistan.
  • 1971 - Hoodbhoy graduated with a double BSc in Electrical Engineering and Mathematics from MIT, USA.
  • 1973 - Double BSc was followed by an MSc in physics with a concentration in solid-state physics
  • 1975 - Hoodbhoy collaborated with Pakistan's leading theoretical physicists Abdus Salam, a Nobel Laureate. The Abdus Salam International Centre for Theoretical Physics (ICTP) is an international research institute for physical and mathematical sciences that operates under a tripartite agreement between the Italian Government, United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO), and International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA).
  • 1978 - Hoodbhoy received a PhD in Nuclear Physics from MIT.
  • 1984 - Received "The Abdus Salam Award" (sometimes called the Salam Prize), which is the most prestigious award that is awarded annually to Pakistani nationals in the fields of chemistry, mathematics, physics, biology.
  • 1985 - Hoodbhoy served as a visiting professor at the Carnegie Mellon University.
  • 1992 - Hoodbhoy published critically acclaimed book "Islam and Science: Religious Orthodoxy and the Battle for Rationality".
  • 1999 - Hoodbhoy alongwith Ishfaq Ahmad and Riazuddin, played a major and influential role in the establishment of the National Center for Physics (NCP), becoming one of the earliest academics and scientists to join the NCP at its inception.
  • 2003 - He was a signatory of the Humanist Manifesto. Hoodbhoy criticized the partition of India, calling it an "unspeakable tragedy" that "separated people who at one time could live together in peace".
  • 2004 - He produced a 13-part documentary series in Urdu for Pakistan Television on critical issues in education, and two series aimed at popularising science. He also made a documentary film 'Crossing the Lines: Kashmir, Pakistan, India' along with Dr. Zia Mian. These documentaries carry heavy emphasis on the issues of education, public health and scientific revolution in Pakistan.
  • 2005 - At National Center for Physics, Hoodbhoy conducted research on different aspects of particle physics, and pioneered studies in modern physics and its extension to mathematical and nuclear physics.
  • 2006 - Hoodbhoy published a brief mathematical description of Generalized Parton Distributions.
  • 2007 - Hoodbhoy re-published the work of Jens Lyng Peterson the Maldacena conjecture (a conjectured equivalence between a string theory and gravity defined on one space, and a quantum field theory without gravity defined by one or less dimension) where he contributed mathematically to the theory.
  • 2010 - Received the Joseph A. Burton Forum Award from the American Physical Society.
  • 2011 - Hoodbhoy joined LUMS while also working as a researcher with Princeton University and as copa columnist with the Express Tribune.
  • 2011 - He was included in the list of 100 most influential global thinkers by Foreign Policy.
  • 2013 - He was made a member of the UN Secretary General's advisory board on Disarmament.
  • 2013 - Dr.Hoodbhoy joined the faculty of Lahore's Forman Christian College after serving LUMS for 2 year and Quaid-e-Azam University, Islamabad for 30 years.
  • 2021 - Dr. Hoodbhoy Started a Youtube Channel, The Black Hole. TBH is a non-profit, open-to-all, casual educational and intellectual space for science, art, and culture.
